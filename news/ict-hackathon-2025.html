ICT & Innovation Hackathon 2025

  1. Home
  2. News & Events
  3. ICT Hackathon
Workshop November 12, 2025 ICT Department 5 min read

Software Development students at St Peter College competed in an intense 24-hour hackathon, using technology and creativity to solve real problems facing local communities in Muhanga District.

The Challenge

Held on November 11–12, 2025, the ICT & Innovation Hackathon brought together 48 Software Development students organised into 12 teams of four. Each team was given a community problem statement and challenged to build a functional prototype — a web app, mobile app, or automated system — within 24 hours.

The problem statements were provided by community organisations in Muhanga District, including a local health clinic, a farmers' cooperative, and a primary school, ensuring that solutions had real-world relevance and potential for deployment.

Problem Statements

  • Health: A mobile app to help patients track medication schedules at Muhanga Health Centre
  • Agriculture: A web platform for farmers to record crop data and get market price updates
  • Education: An SMS-based attendance tracking system for primary schools with no internet
  • Finance: A simple bookkeeping tool for village savings and loan groups (SACCOs)

"We barely slept, but we built something real. When the health clinic manager saw our app, she asked if they could actually use it. That moment made all the hard work worth it."

— Niyonkuru David, Team MediTrack Lead Developer

Next Steps

The three winning teams will receive mentorship support from the ICT Department and a local tech company to refine their prototypes for potential real-world deployment. The college is also exploring opportunities to patent the most viable solutions through the Rwanda Development Board's innovation programme.

The hackathon is now an annual event at St Peter College, reflecting the institution's commitment to innovation, problem-solving, and applying technology for community development.
