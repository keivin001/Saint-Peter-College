National Best College Award 2025

  1. Home
  2. News & Events
  3. Best College Award
Achievement December 1, 2025 Administration Office 4 min read

St Peter College of Shyogwe has been honoured with the National Best College Award 2025 by the Rwanda Education Board, recognising outstanding academic performance, student welfare, and institutional excellence.

A Historic Recognition

On December 1, 2025, during the National Education Awards Ceremony held in Kigali, St Peter College received the prestigious Best College Award in the category of Best Technical and Vocational Education Training (TVET) Institution in the Southern Province.

The award was presented by the Minister of Education to Principal Dr. Jean Bosco, who accepted on behalf of the entire St Peter College community — students, staff, parents, and alumni.

Criteria for the Award

  • Highest graduation rate in the Southern Province (97.3%)
  • Zero reported student welfare incidents in 2025
  • Best infrastructure improvement record among TVET institutions
  • Highest graduate employment rate within 6 months of graduation
  • Top score in the Rwanda Education Board's Quality Assurance inspection

"This award belongs to every student who worked hard, every teacher who went the extra mile, and every parent who trusted us with their child's future. We are humbled and more motivated than ever."

— Dr. Jean Bosco, Principal, accepting the award

What This Means for Students

For current and prospective students, this recognition is a powerful signal: a St Peter College diploma carries weight. Employers across Rwanda increasingly recognise the college's graduates as disciplined, skilled, and work-ready — and this award further reinforces that reputation.

The college has already announced plans to use the associated prize money to establish a new scholarship fund for academically outstanding students from low-income backgrounds, ensuring that excellence remains accessible to all.

Looking Ahead

Building on this recognition, St Peter College has set ambitious targets for 2026, including introducing two new programs, expanding the student body by 15%, and strengthening partnerships with industry for internship placements. The award is not a destination — it is a milestone on a longer journey of excellence.
